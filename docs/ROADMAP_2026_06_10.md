## The strategic question

We've been heads-down on coverage + infrastructure. The product is good enough that the next bottleneck for revenue is **customer signal**, not feature completeness.

> *"More dangerous"* in the rest of this doc means **high-leverage, customer-changing**, not "complex" or "risky." Phase 2 perf was safe and incremental. Putting it in a paying customer's hands is dangerous.

## Three "dangerous" paths, ranked

### 🔥 1. Put it in front of a real buyer THIS week

Highest information value. Zero engineering cost.

**The play:**
- Identify 3-5 acquisitions analysts at storage developers in NJ / PA / DC-metro geographies (LinkedIn, prior contacts, broker intros)
- Give them prod URL + 15-minute demo
- Watch the session live (Loom / Zoom share) or get unfiltered feedback within 24h
- **Their feedback list = the priority list for the next sprint**

**Why this beats more code:**
- We've been guessing about client geography for 3 weeks
- We've been guessing about whether the map is "fast enough"
- We've been guessing about whether multi-county portfolio matters more than per-county polish
- A real buyer surfaces the actual answers in 30 minutes

**Decision gate:** *Do we have at least one acquisitions analyst we can show this to in the next 7 days?* If yes, this is the move. If no, fall to #2 or #3.

---

### 🔥 2. Build the deal-pipeline workflow

Current product shape: *"look at one county dashboard at a time."* That's not what an acquisitions analyst does.

What an analyst actually does: *scan a defined territory daily, get alerts on new fits, share candidates with their team.*

**Required pieces (most already exist in fragments):**
- **Multi-county BuyBox** — one BuyBox spans all 17 operational jurisdictions, not one per county
- **Cross-county dashboard at `/dashboard`** (no jobId in URL) — all parcels matching your BuyBox across every operational geography
- **Daily digest as headline product** — each user gets a digest of NEW deals matching their criteria, deduped against what they've already seen
- **Save-to-shortlist + share workflow** — collaborative shortlists, simple sharing URL
- **Per-user email recipient** — current digest is single-recipient (`settings.digest_default_recipient`); blocks team workflow

**What changes for the customer:** product goes from "zoning data lookup" to "acquisitions pipeline." Different category of product, different price point.

**Cost estimate:** ~1-2 weeks of frontend + small backend. Touches multiple existing surfaces.

**Why this is more dangerous than #3:**
- #3 makes the existing experience faster
- #2 makes the product *be a different thing*
- Customers buy product categories, not load times

---

### 🔥 3. Phase 3 vector tiles + portfolio map

The map still feels slow because 3.5s is fine but not instant. Vector tiles are industry standard for parcels-at-scale.

**Required pieces:**
- Pre-generate vector tiles server-side per jurisdiction (PostGIS `ST_AsMVT` or `tippecanoe`)
- Browser fetches only viewport tiles at current zoom level — milliseconds, not seconds
- Initial load < 500ms across all 17 jurisdictions
- Pan / zoom feels native because tiles are pre-cached at multiple zoom levels

**Plus portfolio map (bundled here):**
- One map view showing all parcels matching the user's BuyBox across all operational jurisdictions
- Acquisitions analysts pan across their entire territory in one map
- Cross-county clustering at low zoom, parcel detail at high zoom

**Cost estimate:** ~3-5 days backend + frontend.

**Why this is dangerous:** map becomes the workflow. Right now the dashboard is a sequence of single-county views. A portfolio map turns territory-wide scanning into a single gesture.

**When this is the right call instead of #2:**
- If customer feedback says "the map is the product"
- If the buyer's workflow is already territory-scan-based
- If you want to nail the existing surface before adding new ones
