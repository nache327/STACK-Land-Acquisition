# Session C exceptions — CT marquee wealth towns (batch1)

## Stamford — M-D Designed Industrial District (5 parcels) — #57 vs #58 tension
Not grounded as a needle (left as existing prohibited stub). Genuine ambiguity:
- **§9.H.2 [Permitted Uses]** for M-D is an enumerated list of Experimental Electronic
  Laboratories + a long list of office types + accessory uses. **Self-Storage Facility is NOT
  named** → #58 closed-list would prohibit.
- BUT **§9.H.3.d [Standards]** affirmatively sets Floor Area Ratio for "a **self-storage facility**
  that does not provide direct accessible ground floor storage units" within an M-D district of the
  §9.H.1.f subtype, and §9.H.4 adds "[Additional Permitted Uses]". M-D bulk is otherwise "governed
  by requirements of the M-G General Industrial District." → #57 affirmative-provision suggests
  self-storage IS contemplated in (at least the 9.H.1.f subtype of) M-D.
- **Decision:** conservative — left prohibited (5 parcels, immaterial to the 72-needle count).
  Escalating for Nache/coordinator: if M-D self-storage should be CONDITIONAL (special permit),
  it adds a handful of parcels at most. Source: Stamford Zoning Regulations web version 08/31/2021.

## Currency notes (Hudson-trap discipline)
- **Stamford**: grounded on the full-text web version dated 08/31/2021 (boardofreps.org). Current is

  (amendments 214-10, 216-26). Verdict is bedrock industrial (by-right in M-G/M-L) — negligible risk.
- **Westport**: grounded on the Effective-04/01/2022 full text (portal.ct.gov). Current online is
  04/12/2024. No-op verdict; a 2024 amendment adding a self-storage district to a wealthy town would
  be a major, unlikely change — negligible risk to a no-op.
